FREQUENTLY ASK QUESTION

What is SR&ED?

The Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) is a federal incentive program to encourage Canadian businesses to conduct research and development (R&D) in Canada. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) is in charge of the SR&ED program, proceeding more than 20,000 claimants and $3 billion tax incentives every year.

How much refund can I expect?

In addition to the federal SR&ED program, some provinces provide extra SR&ED incentives. Generally, a Canadian-controlled private corporation (CCPC) could obtain up to 64% of claimants for salary and material expenses.

What kind of works qualify for the tax incentives?

The works qualify as SR&ED if you are doing a systematic investigation or search, through experiment or analysis to overcome current technological challenges or uncertainty where you canâ€™t get the solution from your existing scientific or technological knowledge base. The works include new products, processes, material, improvement of existing ones, or research reports.

What kind of costs qualify?

The common qualified expenditures include salary, material and subcontractor costs, which directly involve SR&ED eligible work.

Am I qualified if my company is not a tech company?

Most business owners might have misunderstood the SR&ED program and considered themselves disqualified. In fact, the program is for all sectors of business and many non-tech companies successfully got tax refunds from the government.

How do I apply for SR&ED?

1. Identify eligible projects and to collect related documentation in support of SR&ED claims. 2. Draft a strategy to maximize SR&ED claim and optimize eligible financial expenses. 3. Prepare technical reports and tax schedules based on the CRA requirements. 4. File the SR&ED claim with your corporate tax return.

How long does it take to receive my funds?

Assuming you submit your SR&ED claim within a six-month time frame after your fiscal year-end, the following processing times apply upon receipt of a complete claim: Refundable claims which have been accepted as filed will be processed within 3-4 months of the date of receipt. Refundable claims selected for review will be completed within 180 calendar days of the date of receipt.

Will it affect my company if the application failed?

The failed application will not affect your company in any field. You still have a chance to approve your project.

Why does my company need to apply to this program?

The tax incentives help Canadian companies continue, grow or expand your businesses. Especially during the time of COVID19, you will need this extra fund to deal with the challenging situation.
